// PROGRAM OVERVIEW

Operation Green Light is a classified CIA intelligence designation for a covert Soviet nuclear deployment program, active since 1958. The program, orchestrated by Soviet intelligence operative known only as PERSEUS, involves the clandestine positioning of W54 tactical nuclear devices — designated Greenlight bombs — at 43 strategic locations across Western Europe.

The devices are designed for simultaneous remote detonation via an encrypted satellite uplink, triggered from a single primary command terminal whose location remains unknown to CIA. Each device carries a yield sufficient to eliminate a major population center within its blast radius. Combined detonation would render Western Europe uninhabitable and effectively end NATO's strategic capacity.

// DEVICE IDENTIFICATION — W54 GREENLIGHT BOMB

The W54 Special Atomic Demolition Munition (SADM), designated Greenlight bomb in operational context, is a man-portable nuclear device developed by the U.S. Atomic Energy Commission. Perseus has obtained technical schematics and — according to BELL — has acquired modified Soviet variants based on stolen W54 design parameters.

Each device measures approximately 123 cm in length and consists of a cylindrical primary housing, dual-lock arming mechanism, and a Pu-239 / U-233 composite fission core. The Soviet modifications include a remote arming receiver and tamper-proof detonation circuit that cannot be bypassed without the primary abort authentication code.
